Overview

I am on the academic job market seeking a tenure-track assistant professor position. I am PhD candidate at Rutgers University expecting to graduate in May 2019. I work at the intersection of programming languages, software engineering, and high performance computing. My research strives to assist programmers in writing correct and scalable parallel programs.

During my PhD, I proposed light-weight techniques to diagnose correctness and performance problems in a broad spectrum of parallel programs ranging from programs that run on multi-core commodity systems to large scale high performance computing applications. In my dissertation research, I tackle the problem of identifying performance issues that do not manifest when executing the program in a small-scale test or development environment but occur when executed at scale in a production environment.

Going forward, I am interested in exploring hardware extensions, programming interfaces and developer tools to tune software to make best use of such diverse computing systems.

During my graduate career at Rutgers, I had the opportunity to serve as the teaching assistant to a variety of courses. Given my reseach background, I would like to teach courses in programming languages, software testing and debugging, and parallel programming.
